Protein Brownies

These protein brownies are a tasty treat that fits into a PCOS-friendly diet.

The prep time is 5 minutes and the cook time is 20 minutes!
You can cook it for 5-10 more minutes if the middle is raw.
Poke the sides and middle with a fork to see if any batter sticks to it.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup whole wheat flour or 1 cup almond flour
  • 1 scoop protein powder (chocolate works best!)
  • 2 tbsp cocoa powder
  • 2/3 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/3 cup coconut oil
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup dark chocolate chips (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Mix all dry ingredients in a bowl, and wet ingredients in another. Combine.
  3. Pour the batter into a baking dish, preferably an 8-inch pan.
  4. Bake for 20-25 minutes.
  5. Let cool before cutting into squares. Store in refridgerator

Nutritional Facts

Nutrient Amount per serving
Calories 154
Protein 12.6g
Carbohydrates 4g
Total Fat 11g

Apple & Carrot Muffins

These calorie dense and protein rich muffins are filling and a healthy snack when you're in a rush!

The prep time is 20 minutes and the cook time is 25 minutes!
You can cook it for 5-7 more minutes if the middle is raw.
Poke the sides and middle with a fork to see if any batter sticks to it.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1 cup old fashioned oats
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp fine sea salt
  • 8 tablespoons of coconut oil
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 cup grated Granny Smith apple (about 2 medium apples)
  • 1 cup peeled and grated carrots (3 medium carrots or 10 baby carrots)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Mix all dry ingredients in a bowl, and wet ingredients in another. Combine.
  3. Pour the batter into a 12-cup muffin tin lined with parchment paper.
  4. Bake for 25-30 minutes. Insert a knife into the centre of the muffin and ensure it comes out clean before removing it from the oven.
  5. Let cool completely before storing. Store in refridgerator for a week, or up to 3 weeks in the freezer.

Nutritional Facts

Nutrient Amount per serving
Calories 304
Protein 8.2g
Carbohydrates 27.4g
Total Fat 18.8g

Banana Muffins

These calorie dense muffins are filling and a healthy snack for work!

The prep time is 10 minutes and the cook time is 35 minutes!

Ingredients:

  • 1 and 3/4 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 cup old fashioned oats
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup of almond milk or water
  • 1 cup packed mashed ripe bananas (about 3 bananas)
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/3 cup melted coconut oil
  • 2 eggs, preferably at room temperature

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, beat the coconut oil and honey together with a whisk. Add the eggs and beat well. Mix in the mashed bananas and milk, followed by the baking soda, vanilla extract, salt and cinnamon.
  3. Add the flour and oats to the bowl and mix with a large spoon, just until combined.
  4. Divide the batter evenly between the muffin cups, filling each cup about two-thirds full. Sprinkle the tops of the muffins with a small amount of oats (about 1 tablespoon in total). Bake muffins for 22 to 25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into a muffin comes out clean.
  5. Let cool completely before storing. Store in refridgerator for a week, or up to 3 weeks in the freezer.

Calories 208
Protein 4.5g
Carbohydrates 33g
